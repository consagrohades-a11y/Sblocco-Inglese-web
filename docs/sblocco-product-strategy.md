# Sblocco Inglese — Product Strategy & Direction

_Last updated: 2026-06-27_

## North Star

Sblocco Inglese is not just an English course website. It is a practical English-learning platform for Italian adults who need to use English in real situations.

The long-term goal is to build a web app that can work almost independently from live courses, while still integrating those courses in a commercially powerful way.

Core promise:

> Sblocco Inglese diagnoses what blocks your English, gives you a practical path, and trains you to use English in real situations.

The platform should:
1. diagnose the learner's real blockers;
2. give a clear learning path;
3. train through exercises, trainers, repetition, and real-life scenarios;
4. recommend the right paid path when human guidance is useful;
5. support courses, audits, and premium 1:1 work.

---

## The Three-Way Strategy

### 1. Web App / Platform

The platform is the scalable asset.

It should include:
- grammar diagnostics;
- level-based grammar paths;
- vocabulary and expression trainers;
- SRS repetition;
- business English trainers;
- hospitality English trainers;
- interview preparation;
- speaking prompts;
- progress tracking;
- diagnostic feedback;
- personalized recommendations.

The platform should make the student feel:

> I know exactly what is wrong and what I need to do next.

### 2. Courses / Guided Paths

Courses provide structure, deadlines, explanation, correction, and accountability.

They should be practical paths, not generic English courses:
- Fluency Foundations;
- Business English;
- Interview English;
- Hospitality English;
- Advanced English Lab.

Courses should be integrated with the platform, not separate from it.

### 3. Premium Human Support

Human support is the high-value layer.

Possible offers:
- €39 English audit;
- interview sprint;
- business English package;
- hospitality English package;
- fluency reset;
- premium 1:1 path;
- advanced C1/C2 correction lab.

The app handles structure and repetition. Human support handles correction, confidence, nuance, and transformation.

## Positioning

Do not position Sblocco Inglese as generic “English courses.”

Position it as:

> A practical English platform for Italian adults who need English in real situations.

Main audiences:
- people who understand English but freeze when speaking;
- workers who need English professionally;
- people preparing interviews;
- hospitality/tourism workers;
- students or adults who want to finally understand their level and gaps.

The website should ask:

> Where do you get blocked?

Not only:

> What level are you?

## Level Philosophy

Levels should be defined by output ability, not only by grammar topics.

The question is:

> What can the learner actually do by the end of this level?

### A1 — First Speaking Control

A1 must enable first speaking, not just grammar recognition.

Goal:

> By the end of A1, the student can create simple spoken sentences, ask and answer basic questions, and survive simple interactions.

A1 should include:
- be: am / is / are;
- subject pronouns;
- possessives;
- present simple basics;
- questions with be;
- questions with do / does;
- negatives: don’t / doesn’t, isn’t / aren’t;
- have / have got;
- there is / there are;
- can / can’t;
- would like as a useful chunk;
- imperatives;
- articles and plurals;
- this / that / these / those;
- basic prepositions;
- basic adjectives;
- and / but / because.

A1 speaking outcomes:
- introduce yourself;
- say where you are from;
- say what you do;
- talk about routine;
- say what you like, want, and need;
- ask and answer basic questions;
- use numbers, time, and prices;
- make basic requests;
- explain simple problems.

Outcome:

> I can speak in short but usable sentences.

### A2 — Everyday Communication

Goal:

> The student can talk about daily life, past events, future plans, needs, preferences, and simple problems.

Focus:
- past simple;
- future plans;
- requests;
- travel;
- appointments;
- restaurants;
- shopping;
- simple opinions;
- comparing options;
- simple problem-solving.

Outcome:

> I can participate in common everyday conversations.

### B1 — Independent Conversation

Goal:

> The student can maintain a conversation without collapsing.

Focus:
- storytelling;
- opinions;
- experiences;
- explanations;
- present perfect;
- past continuous;
- conditionals;
- used to;
- linking ideas;
- longer answers;
- speaking without translating every sentence.

Outcome:

> I can communicate independently, but I still need more accuracy and confidence.

### B2 — Professional Independence

Goal:

> The student can use English in work situations without sounding basic or confused.

Focus:
- meetings;
- calls;
- emails;
- interviews;
- explaining work;
- giving opinions;
- handling problems;
- presenting ideas;
- professional vocabulary;
- accuracy under pressure.

Outcome:

> I can function professionally in English.

### C1 — Precision and Authority

Goal:

> The student can express complex ideas clearly, naturally, and professionally.

Focus:
- nuance;
- persuasion;
- advanced grammar;
- softening;
- argumentation;
- professional writing;
- leadership language;
- advanced conversation;
- domain-specific English.

Outcome:

> I sound competent, articulate, and credible.

### C2 — Style, Nuance, and High-Level Performance

Goal:

> The student can adapt English to high-stakes contexts with precision and sophistication.

Focus:
- rhetoric;
- register control;
- idiomatic precision;
- cultural nuance;
- executive communication;
- debate;
- advanced writing;
- advanced listening nuance;
- style correction.

Outcome:

> I do not just speak correctly. I control tone, implication, and effect.

C2 should likely be a premium/human-supported layer rather than a cheap self-study product.

## Applied Tracks

The platform should have levels and applied tracks.

```text
Core English Level System:
A1 → A2 → B1 → B2 → C1 → C2

Applied Tracks:
- Fluency
- Business
- Interview
- Hospitality
- Travel / relocation
- Advanced communication
```

Examples:
- “I am A2 and work in a hotel.” → A2 Core + Hospitality Track.
- “I am B1 and need interviews.” → B1 Core + Interview Track.
- “I am B2 but sound basic at work.” → B2/C1 Business + Advanced Expression Trainer.

## Main Product Paths

### Fluency Foundations

Audience: A1–B1.

Promise:

> Start speaking without translating every sentence.

Includes:
- sentence building;
- grammar checkpoints;
- active vocabulary;
- speaking prompts;
- micro-dialogues;
- basic fluency training.

### Business English

Audience: B1–C1.

Promise:

> Use English clearly and professionally at work.

Includes:
- meetings;
- emails;
- calls;
- explaining tasks;
- giving opinions;
- presentations;
- client communication;
- workplace vocabulary.

### Interview English

Audience: B1–C1/C2.

Promise:

> Answer interview questions with structure, confidence, and natural English.

Includes:
- self-introduction;
- strengths and weaknesses;
- STAR method;
- career story;
- relocation;
- behavioral questions;
- role-specific vocabulary;
- professional tone.

### Hospitality English

Audience: A2–B2/C1.

Promise:

> Speak with tourists, guests, clients, and customers without freezing.

This is strategically important in Italy because tourism is strong and Preply students have already shown demand for this kind of practical offer.

Includes:
- hotel reception;
- check-in / check-out;
- restaurant service;
- wine and food explanation;
- allergies and dietary needs;
- bookings;
- complaints;
- directions;
- phone calls;
- payment issues;
- recommendations;
- polite service language;
- wedding/event English;
- problem solving with guests.

Possible name:

> Sblocco Hospitality

### Advanced English Lab

Audience: B2–C2.

Promise:

> Move from correct English to powerful English.

Includes:
- nuance;
- argumentation;
- writing;
- persuasion;
- advanced speaking;
- leadership communication;
- high-level correction.

## Diagnostic Strategy

Diagnostics should be based on problems, not just levels.

### Main Diagnostic

Name:

> English Blocker Diagnostic

Finds:
- approximate level;
- grammar weakness;
- sentence-control weakness;
- speaking blocker;
- vocabulary range;
- confidence/use-case.

### Business Diagnostic

Finds:
- meeting language;
- email clarity;
- explaining tasks;
- professional tone;
- vocabulary precision;
- sentence complexity.

### Interview Diagnostic

Finds:
- self-introduction clarity;
- STAR method readiness;
- grammar under pressure;
- career vocabulary;
- answer structure;
- confidence gaps.

### Hospitality Diagnostic

Finds:
- reception/service vocabulary;
- polite requests;
- problem-solving phrases;
- customer interaction ability;
- listening/response readiness;
- grammar needed for service.

Each diagnostic should recommend a path.

## Self-Selling Mechanism

The website sells itself when it creates this experience:

1. User arrives with vague frustration.
2. The site makes the problem specific.
3. The diagnostic confirms the problem.
4. The result gives a clear path.
5. Free exercises give a small win.
6. Paid support feels like the logical next step.

The site should not scream “buy my course.”

It should make the user think:

> This finally shows me what is wrong with my English.

Example result:

> Your main issue is not vocabulary. Your sentence control drops when you need to answer quickly. Start B1 Fluency Foundations or book the audit.

Hospitality example:

> You know basic English, but you lack service phrases, polite structures, and problem-solving language. Start Hospitality English A2/B1.

## Business Model

### Phase 1 — Fast Monetization

Sell:
- €39 audit;
- €99–199 self-study or mini-course;
- €149–249 guided mini-course;
- 1:1 package when needed.

Best early candidate:

> Hospitality English Starter Path

Why:
- specific audience;
- strong Italian market;
- clear pain;
- easier content creation;
- easier Facebook/local group marketing;
- less generic than business English;
- validated by Preply student demand.

### Phase 2 — Membership / Platform

Add:
- €19–39/month membership;
- paid trainer access;
- full grammar path;
- progress dashboard;
- path recommendations.

### Phase 3 — Higher Scale

Add:
- cohort courses;
- C1/C2 advanced lab;
- corporate packages;
- teacher dashboard later if useful.

## Build Priority

Do not build all A1–C2 content immediately.

Build the engine first:
- levels;
- topics;
- tracks;
- tests;
- tags;
- diagnostics;
- recommendations;
- progress;
- CTAs.

Then build one complete vertical slice.

Best first slice:

> A2/B1 Hospitality + Speaking

This gives a concrete commercial product and validates the platform model.

---

## What We Must Not Forget

- Sblocco Inglese is a platform + courses + premium support system.
- It should not become a random collection of pages.
- Levels must be practical and output-based.
- A1 must enable first speaking, not just grammar recognition.
- Hospitality is a major commercial track.
- Diagnostics are the selling engine.
- The site should route users by their blocker and use-case.
- The platform should always tell the learner what to do next.
- Human support remains premium and valuable.
